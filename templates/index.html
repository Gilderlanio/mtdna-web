The mtDNA mutation-cancer network

We constructed a bipartite network of mutations and cancer based on TCMA and mtDNA analysis by Cavalcante et al. (2019) datasets. The mutation-cancer network is a graph G(V, E), where V comprises two distinct sets of nodes: mitochondrial mutations (u) and cancers (v).

Data!

For interactive and visualization analysis, our tool includes data from the following references:

  • Cavalcante, Giovanna Chaves, et al. "Whole mitochondrial genome sequencing highlights mitochondrial impact in gastric cancer." Scientific reports 9.1 (2019): 1-13.

  • Yuan, Y.; Ju, Y.S.; Kim, Y.; Li, J.; Wang, Y.; Yoon, C.J.; Yang, Y.; Martincorena, I.; Creighton, C.J.; Weinstein, J.N.; et al. Comprehensive molecular characterization of mitochondrial genomes in human cancers. Nat. Genet. 2020, 52, 342–352, doi:10.1038/s41588-019-0557-x.

    • The mtDNA mutation-cancer network is developed by the Laboratory of Human and Medical Genetics (LGHM) at the Institute of Biological Sciences from the Federal University of Pará, Belém, Pará, Brazil.
